## How this reshapes the database

Adding the 12 user-proposed sectors plus 6 newly identified sectors brings the total from **16 to 34 categories**. The research identified approximately **350+ companies** that should be evaluated for inclusion, which would bring the database from 463 to potentially **750–800 companies** — a 60–70% expansion.

The most capital-intensive gaps are defense AI ($49.1B in 2025 funding), GPU cloud neoclouds (Nscale, Nebius, Crusoe collectively worth $50B+), and AI developer tools ($5.2B raised in 2025 alone). The most company-dense gaps are vertical AI applications (healthcare alone has 30+ significant players) and marketing/sales AI (24+ companies). The most strategically important gaps are vector databases (infrastructure dependency for every RAG system), agent orchestration (foundational for the agentic AI wave), and AI safety (where $1.58B in acquisitions signals rapid sector maturation).

The critical next step is integrating these findings into the database schema, assigning consistent sub-sector classifications, and establishing a regular refresh cadence — given that this ecosystem saw **$84B in mega-rounds** and dozens of major acquisitions in 2025 alone, the landscape shifts materially every quarter.
